In detail

Each provider, plainly

EBOX — symmetric fibre, no upload compromise

EBOX runs on Bell's FTTH fibre network. It's the only one of the three to offer true symmetry: 150/150, 500/500, and 1000/750 on the Gbps tier. Recommended by Protégez-Vous since 2017. Quebec grid: $49 (150 Mbps), $55 (500), $65 (1 Gbps), equipment and installation included, no contract. The referral code GE911 gives a $25 credit on your 2nd bill. Available in both Quebec and Ontario. See EBOX plans.

Fizz — cheapest floor, but cable and Quebec-only

Fizz (Vidéotron) runs on the cable network, asymmetric: upload caps at 50 Mbps even on the 940 Mbps plan. Grid: $40 (30 Mbps) up to $68 (940). Wi-Fi 5 modem on entry plans, Wi-Fi 6 from 400 Mbps up. Fully online (no phone support), loyalty-credit system. Important: Fizz internet is Quebec-only, not available in Ontario. See Fizz plans.

oxio — simple and well rated, but cable only

oxio (Cogeco) runs on the Vidéotron network in Quebec (wholesale access). Simple interface, 60-day guarantee, no price hikes, well-rated service (~4.4/5 PlanHub, 4.2/5 Google). The key point: oxio's price and speed vary enormously by address. In Montreal (and Brossard), we measured cable with no 1 Gbps, and the 500 Mbps plan at $75 — more expensive than EBOX fibre ($55). But in Trois-Rivières, the same speed drops to $40 and 1 Gbps is offered at $50 — there, oxio beats EBOX on price (EBOX only keeps the symmetric upload). In all cases the upload stays throttled (2 to 50 Mbps depending on plan and area, asymmetric cable). oxio markets "up to 1 Gbps" for download: always check your postal code — it's the only one of the three where the price shifts this much. FAQ

Frequently asked questions

EBOX or Fizz: which is cheaper?
It depends on the speed. Fizz has the lowest entry price with its 30 Mbps plan at $40, ideal for 1 to 2 people. But at equal speed from 150 Mbps up, EBOX is the better value: equipment and installation at $0, and symmetric fibre. At 500 Mbps, EBOX is $55 versus $58 at Fizz, with 10 times more upload.
Is oxio reliable?
oxio is well rated for customer service (about 4.4/5 on PlanHub, 4.2/5 on Google) and offers a 60-day guarantee. In Quebec its infrastructure runs on the Vidéotron network (cable). The key point: its price varies enormously by address. The same 500 Mbps plan costs $75 in Montreal but $40 in Trois-Rivières. Always check your postal code before concluding anything about oxio.
Does Fizz internet work outside Quebec?
No. Fizz Internet is offered in Quebec only — it is not available in Ontario. Within Quebec, availability depends on the Vidéotron cable network, so a postal-code check is required before subscribing. If you are in Ontario, EBOX (Bell FTTH) is the relevant option from this comparison.
Who owns EBOX, Fizz and oxio?
EBOX is owned by Bell and uses Bell's FTTH fibre network. Fizz is a Vidéotron brand and uses Vidéotron's cable network. oxio is owned by Cogeco but, in Quebec, runs on the Vidéotron network (TPIA wholesale access). All three are brands tied to large operators — a useful transparency point.
Are there contracts with EBOX, Fizz or oxio?
None of the three lock you into a fixed-term contract. EBOX and oxio are commitment-free; Fizz is prepaid. oxio adds a 60-day guarantee.
Which provider is best for gaming?
EBOX, for a specific reason: it is the only one of the three on symmetric fibre (500/500, 1 Gbps at 1000/750). Fizz and oxio are on Vidéotron cable, with upload capped at 50 Mbps. For online gaming, streaming or large uploads, EBOX's symmetric upload makes the difference wherever its fibre is available.
