In detail

Each provider in plain words

EBOX — Bell's fibre, without Bell's price

EBOX is owned by Bell and runs on its FTTH fibre network: identical physical performance where fibre is deployed. Symmetrical fibre (150/150, 500/500, 1000/750), recommended by Protégez-Vous since 2017. Quebec grid: $49 (150 Mbps), $55 (500), $65 (1 Gbps), equipment and installation included, no contract, stable grid. Referral code GE911 gives a $25 credit on the 2nd invoice. See EBOX plans.

Bell — the speed ceiling, at the price of expiring credits

Bell Fibe covers Quebec in FTTH with the widest range and the highest speed ceiling (3 Gbps symmetrical at the tested address, up to 8 Gbps depending on area), Wi-Fi 7 pod included. Promo prices checked July 10, 2026: Fibe 150 at $60, Fibe 500 at $60, Gigabit 1.5 at $70, Gigabit 3.0 at $75 — autopay credit included. The thing to watch, in Bell's own words: "Prices may increase during subscription." These are limited-time promotions, not a grid: the regular rate is higher and the gap with EBOX widens when the credits expire — for the same physical network. See Bell plans.

Videotron — the local player, strong on ecosystem, weak on upload

Videotron operates its own network in Quebec — cable (Helix) in most residential areas. Grid checked July 10, 2026 (Montreal South Shore area): 100 Mbps at $68 (upload 30), 500 at $75 (upload 50), GIGA (1 Gbps) at $80 (upload 50) — "lifetime discount" and equipment rental included. The weak spot is clear: upload caps at 50 Mbps even on GIGA, where EBOX gives 750 Mbps for $15 less. Its real strengths: permanent discounts rather than expiring credits, the Helix ecosystem (Helix Fi app, parental controls) and bundle savings with mobile and TV. Its entry level stays expensive ($68 for 100/30, where EBOX gives 150 Mbps symmetrical at $49). See Videotron plans.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

EBOX or Bell: which one is cheaper?
At comparable speeds, EBOX at grid price — on Bell's own FTTH fibre network. Honest nuance: on promotion, Bell can get close (500 Mbps checked at $60 in July 2026, versus $55 at EBOX). The structural difference: Bell prices are limited-time promos that "may increase during subscription" — Bell's own wording — while the EBOX grid ($49/$55/$65) doesn't go back up.
Does EBOX use the same network as Bell?
Yes. EBOX is owned by Bell and runs on the same FTTH fibre network. Physical performance is identical where fibre is deployed; the difference comes down to price ($10 to $20 less per month), bill stability and a simpler offer. One nuance: EBOX tops out at 1 Gbps (1000/750), while Bell sells tiers up to 8 Gbps symmetrical.
Bell or Videotron in Quebec?
On technology, Bell: symmetrical FTTH fibre, tiers up to 8 Gbps by area, upload of 940 Mbps and above. Videotron runs on cable (Helix) in most areas: upload capped at 30-50 Mbps and 1 Gbps at $80 — pricier than Bell's Gigabit 1.5 ($70 promo checked in July 2026). Videotron's strengths: "lifetime discounts" rather than expiring credits, the Helix ecosystem and mobile/TV bundle savings.
Do Bell prices go up after the promotion?
Yes — Bell writes it itself on its store: "Prices may increase during subscription." Displayed prices are limited-time promotions including conditional credits (e.g. autopay): always check the regular rate before signing. By comparison, EBOX displays a stable grid and Videotron "lifetime discounts" on several plans.
Are there contracts at EBOX, Bell or Videotron?
None of the three imposes a fixed-term contract on residential internet in Quebec. Watch out with Bell: no contract doesn't mean stable price — the displayed prices are limited-time promotions that "may increase during subscription", in Bell's own words.
Which provider for gaming and remote work?
For the best upload per dollar: EBOX (500/500 at $55, 1000/750 at $65, symmetrical fibre). For maximum absolute upload: Bell Gigabit 3.0 or 8.0 (up to 8 Gbps symmetrical by area). Avoid for upload: Videotron on cable, capped at 30-50 Mbps even on GIGA. At equal budget, EBOX has the best upload-to-price ratio.
