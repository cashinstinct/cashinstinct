The true value of a point depends entirely on the redemption method chosen — not on a single marketing number. In 2026, rates range from 0.07¢/point (PC Optimum statement credit) to 2.29¢/point (CIBC Aventura, short-haul airline chart).

📊 Two program families: in-house bank programs with a single rate (Blue Rewards, PC Optimum in-store) are easy to evaluate — no optimization possible. Programs with multiple fixed tiers (TD, NBC, Scene+, BMO Rewards, MBNA) or variable rates (MR, CIBC Aventura) reward optimization — sometimes yielding 2 to 4 times the value of the simplest redemption.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the value of a reward point calculated?

It is calculated by dividing the dollar value obtained by the number of points required, expressed in cents per point. For example, if 1,000 points yield a $10 travel credit, the rate is 1 cent per point. Most programs have multiple redemption methods with different rates — the best rate isn't always the most convenient.

Why does the value of the same point change depending on the redemption method?

Issuers (banks, Amex) set different rates based on where the points are spent: statement credit, gift cards, merchandise catalogs, or transfer to a travel partner. Travel/transfer redemptions generally yield the best rate; gift cards and merchandise catalogs yield the worst.

Which program has the easiest rate to evaluate?

Blue Rewards (BMO) is the only one with a single fixed rate (0.667¢/point) regardless of the redemption method. NBC À la carte Rewards is predictable but not simple: each method has a fixed rate known in advance (1¢ via the travel agency, 0.83¢ for RRSP/TFSA, 0.4¢ for generic statement credit), but there are 5 different tiers rather than just one.

Which program has the highest potential value?

CIBC Aventura, via its airline chart, can reach 2.29 cents per point on short-haul flights — significantly higher than other programs. However, this optimal rate requires booking via the CIBC airline chart rather than taking a statement credit, where the value drops to 0.63 cents.

Are Blue Rewards and BMO Rewards the same thing?

No, they are two distinct BMO programs. Blue Rewards is the new coalition program that replaces AIR MILES (Blue Points earned via debit card or receipt scanning). BMO Rewards is the points program attached to BMO credit cards (like the BMO World Elite Mastercard). The two point currencies are not interchangeable.

Are these rates guaranteed?

No. The displayed rates are verified on the date indicated at the top and bottom of the page using public sources (official issuer websites and independent analyses). Issuers can modify them without notice — always validate the exact rate at the time of redemption on your issuer's website.
