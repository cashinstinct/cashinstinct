Scene+

Scene+ Rewards (Scotiabank)

The Scene+ program is one of the simplest and most flexible for this method, offering a direct cash-out at the optimal rate of 1¢ per point.

  1. Book a refundable hotel room with your Scotiabank credit card (e.g., Scotia Gold Amex).
  2. Log into your Scotia account, navigate to your transactions, and select the Apply Points to Travel option.
  3. Select the hotel transaction (valid for travel purchases in the last 90 days) and apply the desired amount of points.
  4. The statement credit will appear within the following business days.
  5. Cancel the hotel booking through the merchant to receive the cash refund.
BMO Rewards

BMO Rewards

Since the program update (which allows booking travel yourself without going through the BMO agency), the RHT has become the preferred method to cash out points at the maximum rate of 0.67¢/point.

  1. Charge a refundable hotel transaction to your BMO Rewards card.
  2. Within a maximum of **30 days**, log into the BMO Rewards portal and select Pay with points.
  3. Apply your points against this travel transaction. The credit will post to your account within 3 to 5 days.
  4. Cancel the original booking to get the cash refund.
À la carte

NBC À la carte Rewards

The National Bank of Canada (NBC) mechanic uses the "Flexible travel discount" option. It allows you to cash out points (at 0.83¢ or 0.91¢ depending on whether you cross the 55,000 points threshold) without having to deal with their internal agency.

  1. Book a refundable hotel on a third-party site (Expedia, Booking, etc.) so the transaction is categorized as a travel purchase by MasterCard.
  2. Wait for the transaction to officially post to your account (not pending).
  3. Within 60 days following the purchase, log into the NBC Rewards portal, go to the travel section, and select Flexible travel discount.
  4. Apply your points against this specific expense. The statement credit will be issued within a few days.
  5. Cancel the hotel on the third-party site to receive the cash refund.
MBNA Rewards

MBNA Rewards

The RHT is the recommended method for this program because it allows you to get the optimal travel rate of 1¢/point, avoiding MBNA's standard cashback option which reduces the value of the points.

  1. Book a fully refundable hotel on any booking site (Expedia, Booking, or directly with the hotel) with your MBNA card.
  2. Wait for the transaction to change from pending to posted.
  3. Log into the MBNA Rewards portal and select the option to redeem your points for eligible travel purchases (valid for transactions in the last 90 days).
  4. Apply your points against the hotel purchase. The statement credit will be applied within a few days.
  5. Cancel the hotel booking via the merchant to cash out the refund to your card.
Aventura

CIBC Aventura

The CIBC mechanic relies on applying points to a pending transaction.

  1. Book a fully refundable hotel through a third party (Expedia, Booking, etc.). Never go through the CIBC portal.
  2. Monitor your account: as soon as the transaction appears as Pending, the Redeem with points option activates.
  3. Apply your points immediately against this transaction. The option will disappear once the transaction officially posts.
  4. Wait a few days, then cancel the hotel via the third-party site. The refund will be issued in cash directly to your card.
⚠️ Fatal Error: If you book the hotel through the CIBC rewards portal by paying with your points, the cancellation will only refund you in Aventura points. You won't have generated any cash out.
Pro Tip: CIBC Aventura and promotions
The standard RHT for CIBC requires catching the transaction while it is pending to get 1¢/point. However, CIBC sometimes launches Shopping with Points promotions allowing you to erase any purchase at a boosted rate (often 1.25¢/point). When such a promotion is active, the RHT becomes useless: you simply target a regular daily expense to cash out points at a profit.
TD Rewards

TD First Class Travel (FCT)

The TD method requires a Product Switch to block the points from returning.

  1. Book a refundable hotel through the Expedia for TD (E4TD) portal to get the maximum rate.
  2. Apply your TD Rewards points to cover the transaction.
  3. Critical Step: Before doing anything else, request a Product Switch of your FCT card to a TD card that does not use the TD Rewards system (e.g., a Cash Back card).
  4. Once the new card is active in the system, cancel the hotel booking. With no TD Rewards account left to send the points back to, the system will issue a cash credit to the new account.
⚠️ Fatal Error: If you cancel the hotel before the card switch is complete, the points will simply return to your TD Rewards balance.
Annual Credit

Amex Gold / Platinum — Travel Credit

This mechanic does not touch MR points, but allows you to cash out the annual statutory credit.

  1. Book a refundable and prepaid hotel (mandatory) via the Amex Travel portal.
  2. Wait for the transaction and the accompanying $100 (Amex Gold) or $200 (Amex Platinum) credit to appear on the account.
  3. Cancel the booking after a few days. The hotel will refund the transaction amount.
  4. The $100 or $200 credit stays on the account. (Note: Amex Canada generally does not claw back this credit, unlike Amex US's more aggressive practices).
Logistics

What to do with the credit balance?

If your cash-out generates a large positive balance (e.g., your card drops to -$500), you have two options:

  • Organic spending (Recommended): Simply use the card for your daily expenses (groceries, gas, bills) until the balance returns to zero. This is the most discreet and frictionless method.
  • Check request: You can contact the financial institution to request a check or transfer for the excess balance. The success and speed of this method depend entirely on each bank's internal procedures.
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Refundable Hotel Trick (RHT)?

The Refundable Hotel Trick (RHT) is a travel-rewards cash-out method where you book a refundable hotel, redeem points or a travel credit against that charge, then cancel the booking so the refund returns to your card in cash.

Is the Refundable Hotel Trick allowed by banks?

No. These methods generally go against the spirit or terms of the rewards programs. Using them can expose you to points clawbacks, account review, or account closure.

Do all Canadian rewards programs work the same way?

No. Some programs require the hotel charge to be posted before you redeem points, while others only work while the transaction is still pending.

What is the main risk with the RHT?

The main risk is that the issuer may reverse the points redemption, claw back the value received, or close your rewards account if it detects abuse.

Can you use the RHT with travel credits instead of points?

Sometimes. For example, some cardholders use refundable prepaid hotel bookings to trigger annual travel credits, then cancel the reservation after the credit appears.
