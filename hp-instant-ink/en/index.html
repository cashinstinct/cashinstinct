Frequently Asked Questions — HP Instant Ink

Is HP Instant Ink available everywhere in Canada?

Yes. HP Instant Ink is available in every Canadian province and territory, billed in Canadian dollars through hpinstantink.com (there is no separate .ca sign-up site). As long as you have an eligible HP inkjet printer with Wi-Fi, referral code SVdn7 adds 1 free month regardless of where you live in Canada.

Does the code work if I already have free months included with my printer purchase?

Yes — that's the stacking principle. If your new printer comes with 6 free months of HP+, entering code SVdn7 during registration adds a 7th free month right after the others.

Is HP Instant Ink available for laser (toner) printers?

Not for new customers. HP has discontinued new Instant Ink toner subscriptions: per HP's official FAQ, new customers can no longer enroll the LaserJet 100 or 200 series and LaserJet Pro 3000 or 4000 series printers. This affects new sign-ups only — existing toner subscribers keep their current plan. For new enrollments, code SVdn7 applies to inkjet plans only (DeskJet, Envy, OfficeJet, Tango). Source: HP Instant Ink FAQ (Canada).

Which HP printers are eligible for Instant Ink in Canada?

Most HP inkjet printers under 5 years old with a Wi-Fi connection are eligible. This includes the DeskJet, DeskJet Plus, Envy, Envy Photo, Envy Pro, OfficeJet, OfficeJet Pro and Tango series. Excluded: HP Ink Advantage (XL-only) models and LaserJet toner printers (no new enrollment). Confirm your exact model on HP's official compatibility checker — or see our eligible printers section above.

Can I cancel the subscription easily?

Yes, the service has no fixed-term commitment. You can upgrade or downgrade your plan at any time from your profile, or pause the subscription if you no longer need automated ink.

Do we own the ink cartridges provided?

No. Instant Ink cartridges remain the exclusive property of HP. They are loaned as part of your subscription. If you cancel the service, these specific cartridges stop working electronically and must be returned to HP via the provided recycling envelope.

Is a permanent Wi-Fi connection required?

Yes. For the service to work, your printer must remain connected to the Internet via your home Wi-Fi network. This is what allows HP's servers to algorithmically track the number of pages printed and remaining ink level in order to trigger automatic shipments.

How does the rollover of unused pages work?

If you print fewer pages than your monthly plan quota, the remaining pages are not lost. They automatically roll over to the following month. The maximum rollover balance depends on your plan (generally up to two or three times your monthly quota).

What happens if I exceed my monthly plan?

If you exceed your monthly page quota (and have no rolled-over pages in reserve), HP charges a small fee for each additional set of pages. The exact amount depends on your plan and appears automatically on your next invoice — there is nothing to manage manually. If you regularly print more than your plan allows, upgrading to a higher tier is usually cheaper than paying overage fees.

Does printing colour photos use more of my plan?

No — and that is HP Instant Ink's main advantage. A page is a page. Whether you print a single line of black and white text or a full-colour high-definition photo on letter-size paper, it counts as exactly 1 page against your plan.

Can I change my plan up or down mid-subscription?

Yes, the service is fully flexible. You can change your plan at any time from your online dashboard. An upgrade may apply immediately or at the next cycle, while a downgrade will take effect at your next billing period.

What is the difference between an HP promotional code and a referral code?

HP promo codes are temporary advertising keywords tied to marketing events — they expire after a few weeks. A referral code (here SVdn7) is a member identifier that applies a one-free-month invite offer with no fixed expiry. Both may appear on the same sign-up form.
