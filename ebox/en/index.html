Glossary

EBOX terms to understand before ordering

These definitions clarify the labels customers encounter during sign-up and billing.

Referral customer number
An EBOX account identifier used for referrals. The referral code featured on this page is GE911.
Referral code
The common term for the customer number entered in the referral field. For eligible customers, GE911 provides a $25 credit on the second bill.
Promo code
A temporary marketing code issued by EBOX. The Promo Code field is separate from the referral field, so GE911 should not be entered there.
Referral program
EBOX's ongoing program for customers who refer eligible new residential subscribers. The new customer and referrer may each receive a credit under the program terms.
Second bill
The $25 referral credit does not appear on the first invoice. For an eligible order, it is applied during the next billing cycle.
Reactivation
Restoring a former account rather than submitting a new order. EBOX determines whether a returning customer qualifies for the referral program.
New customer
A person opening a new residential EBOX service. A former customer may be treated as new after more than 12 months, but final eligibility is determined by EBOX.
FTTH
Fibre to the home: an optical fibre connection that reaches the residence where this technology is available.
