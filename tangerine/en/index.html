Common Mistakes

Why your Tangerine bonus didn't apply (and how to avoid it)

Most "I didn't get my bonus" cases come down to a handful of avoidable mistakes. Because the $50 Orange Key and the $250 payroll bonus have different conditions, an error on one doesn't void the other — but it's easy to trip on the details. Here are the usual culprits.

⚠️ The two bonuses have separate rules — don't mix them up. The $50 Orange Key needs a $250 balance kept 60 consecutive days (no payroll required). The $250 bonus needs payroll direct deposits of at least $200/month for 2 consecutive months (no Orange Key required). Hitting one condition does not satisfy the other.

$50 Orange Key — why it fails

  • Key entered too late. The Orange Key goes in the "Enter an Orange Key" field at the very first enrollment step — not at checkout, not after the account exists. No retroactive field.
  • Balance dropped below $250. The $250 must stay in the account for 60 consecutive days. Pulling funds out early resets eligibility.
  • Wrong account type. The referral bonus is tied to opening an eligible account as the primary holder — confirm the account you opened qualifies.

$250 payroll bonus — why it fails

  • Deposit isn't a true payroll direct deposit. It must be an eligible recurring payroll (or qualifying recurring) deposit — a manual transfer or e-Transfer from your own account does not count.
  • Under the $200/month floor. Payroll must total at least $200 per month, and continue for at least 2 consecutive months.
  • Set up outside the window. The first eligible payroll deposit must land within 60 days of opening the chequing account, which itself must be opened within 60 days of your Client Number being created.

If you met every condition and still don't see the credit after the stated window, contact Tangerine with your dates — bonuses are paid on a defined schedule, not instantly.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Orange Key 14130944S1 stackable with the $250 bonus?

The two offers use separate mechanisms and may be combined when all of their respective conditions are met. Orange Key 14130944S1 must be entered during enrollment for the $50 referral program. The separate $250 offer depends on qualifying payroll direct deposits and does not require an additional code.

Where exactly do I enter the Orange Key?

In the "Enter an Orange Key" field at the first step of the Tangerine sign-up form — before choosing your account type, before entering personal information, before payment. It is the very first screen of the sign-up process.

How long does it take to receive the $50 bonus?

Timing depends on when you make the initial deposit. You have 60 days to deposit at least $250, must then maintain a minimum $250 balance for 60 consecutive days, and Tangerine may pay the referral bonus within 30 days after all requirements are met, subject to eligibility.

How do I activate the $250 direct deposit bonus?

No additional code is required. Redirect a qualifying payroll direct deposit to Tangerine (institution 614, transit 00152, minimum $200 per month). The first deposit must arrive within 60 days of opening. After qualifying deposits have been received for at least two consecutive months, Tangerine may pay the $250 bonus according to the offer terms.

Is Tangerine available in Quebec?

Yes. Tangerine offers its full banking services in Quebec. The mobile app, website, and customer service are fully available in French.

What are the Tangerine banking codes for direct deposit?

Institution number: 614. Transit number: 00152. Your personal account number is accessible in the Tangerine mobile app, under your account details.

What is a Tangerine Orange Key?

The Orange Key is Tangerine's official term for its referral code. Entering 14130944S1 in the designated field allows you to request the $50 referral offer, subject to all conditions. It is separate from promo codes used for other Tangerine campaigns.

Is Tangerine safe and CDIC insured?

Yes. Tangerine Bank is a member of the Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC) in its own right, with eligible deposits insured up to $100,000 per category, independently of Scotiabank. It is a Schedule I Canadian bank, wholly owned by Scotiabank, and regulated by OSFI and the FCAC.

Does Tangerine have monthly fees?

No. The chequing and savings accounts have $0 monthly fees, no minimum balance, and unlimited free transactions. Situational fees exist (such as a ~$45 NSF fee, $1.50 to $3.00 for non-Scotiabank ABM withdrawals, and $20 for an additional cheque book) and are disclosed up front.

Who owns Tangerine?

Tangerine is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Scotiabank (The Bank of Nova Scotia), one of Canada's Big Five banks. It launched as ING Direct in 1997, was acquired by Scotiabank in 2012, and rebranded to Tangerine in 2014.

Is there a minimum deposit to open a Tangerine account?

No minimum deposit is required to open the account, and there is no minimum balance to maintain. The bonuses have their own funding conditions: a $250 balance kept 60 consecutive days for the $50 Orange Key, and payroll of at least $200 per month for the $250 bonus.

How long does it take to open an account, and what do I need?

Sign-up takes a few minutes in the app: choose Sign Up Digitally, scan a valid government-issued photo ID, take a selfie to match it, and enter your details. You must be a Canadian resident at the age of majority in your province or territory, with a Canadian mailing address.

Is the Tangerine bonus taxable?

The tax treatment of a bank promotion may depend on the nature of the offer and your circumstances. Taxable interest generally must be reported even when no T5 slip is issued. Tangerine, the Canada Revenue Agency, or a qualified tax professional can confirm the treatment that applies to your promotion. This is general information, not tax advice.

What happens if I close the account after getting the bonus?

You must meet all the offer conditions first: for the $50 Orange Key, keeping $250 for 60 consecutive days; for the $250 bonus, payroll for 2 consecutive months. Closing early or pulling funds before the holding period ends can disqualify the bonus. Always read the specific offer terms before closing.

Can I deposit cash, and how do I access ATMs?

Yes. You can deposit cash at Scotiabank and Tangerine ABMs, and deposit cheques in-app with Cheque-In. Withdrawals are free at 3,500+ Scotiabank ABMs in Canada and the EXCHANGE network; other ATMs charge $1.50 in Canada or $3.00 worldwide, which Tangerine does not reimburse.

Does Tangerine have a debit card?

Yes. The Tangerine Client Card with Visa Debit works anywhere Visa is accepted and can be added to Apple Pay, Google Pay, and Samsung Pay. It is covered by Interac and Visa Debit Zero Liability protection against unauthorized purchases.
